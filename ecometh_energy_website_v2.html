How It Works

From Field to
Clean Fuel

A four-step engineered pathway that converts agricultural biomass into commercial-grade Bio-CNG with zero residual waste.

01
🚜

Harvest & Shred

Napier grass is harvested from partner farms and mechanically shredded into optimized smaller pieces for maximum surface area.

02
🧫

Anaerobic Digestion

Biomass is fed into sealed digesters where microorganisms decompose it entirely in the absence of oxygen.

03
⚗️

Biogas Generation

The decomposition process captures and isolates methane-rich raw biogas from the digester system.

04
🔵

Purification & Compression

Raw gas is rigorously purified and compressed into commercial-grade Bio-CNG ready for industrial delivery.

FAQs

Frequently Asked
Questions

What purity standard does your Bio-CNG meet? ▾
Our Bio-CNG meets IS 16087:2016, India's national standard for compressed biogas — the same standard used for commercial CNG. Methane content is ≥90% with CO₂, moisture, and H₂S removed to specified limits, making it suitable for industrial burners, commercial fleets, and boiler applications.
Can Bio-CNG be used as a direct substitute for regular CNG? ▾
Yes. Commercial-grade Bio-CNG (CBG) is chemically equivalent to fossil-derived CNG with the same energy content and pressure characteristics. Existing CNG infrastructure (vehicles, burners, pipelines) can operate on Bio-CNG without any modification.
What is the minimum order quantity for Bio-CNG supply? ▾
We are currently in commercial establishment phase. For enquiries regarding supply agreements, minimum volumes, and delivery logistics, please reach out to us directly via the contact form or WhatsApp — our team will respond within 48 hours.
Is your bio-fertilizer certified for agricultural use? ▾
Our organic bio-fertilizer is produced to comply with the Fertilizer Control Order (FCO) standards for organic manure. It is nutrient-rich, free from synthetic chemicals, and suitable for application across a range of crop types.
Is Ecometh Energy SATAT registered? ▾
Ecometh Energy's project is structurally aligned with the SATAT framework. Our plant specifications, feedstock model, and output standards meet the eligibility criteria for Letter of Intent (LoI) from PSU Oil Marketing Companies. For detailed registration status and documentation, please make an investor enquiry.
What makes the Napier grass feedstock model de-risked? ▾
Napier grass is a perennial crop that can be harvested 4–6 times per year, provides consistent yield regardless of seasonal variation, and is cultivated by partner farms within close proximity to the Anekal plant — reducing logistics risk. Unlike municipal or mixed agricultural waste, the feedstock composition is highly uniform, resulting in predictable gas yield.
Is IREDA or bank financing available for this project? ▾
Bio-CNG projects with SATAT alignment are eligible for IREDA (Indian Renewable Energy Development Agency) project financing as well as term loans from commercial banks under the renewable energy priority sector. MNRE capital subsidy eligibility is also being evaluated for our plant. Detailed financing structure is available in the investor information pack.
Where can I access more detailed financial and technical information? ▾
A Techno-Economic Viability (TEV) report and an investor information memorandum are available to qualified investors upon request. Please submit an investor enquiry via our dedicated investor contact form and one of the founders will respond within 2 business days.

Mission & Vision

Why We Exist

Guided by the conviction that sustainable energy production and responsible agriculture must reinforce each other.

🎯

Our Mission

To produce clean, sustainable alternatives to conventional fossil fuels through environmentally responsible waste utilization — converting Napier grass biomass into commercially valuable Bio-CNG and organic bio-fertilizer, while enriching the farming communities and ecosystems we operate within.

🔭

Our Vision

To establish a commercially scalable, zero-waste circular energy model in Karnataka that demonstrates how agriculture and clean energy can form a self-sustaining loop — reducing India's dependence on fossil fuels while revitalizing soil health and rural livelihoods at scale.
